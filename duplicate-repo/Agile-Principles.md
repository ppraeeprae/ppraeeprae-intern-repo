# Agile Principles Reflection

## Research & Learn
### What is Agile?

Agile is a way of managing projects and developing software that focuses on flexibility and teamwork. Instead of planning everything in detail from the beginning, teams work step by step and improve the product over time.

In Agile, work is usually done in small pieces. After completing each part, the team can review the results, get feedback, and make improvements. This makes it easier to adjust when requirements change.

Compared to traditional project management methods like Waterfall, Agile is more flexible. Traditional methods usually follow a strict plan from start to finish, while Agile allows teams to adapt and make changes along the way.


### Core values and Principles of Agile

The Agile Manifesto highlights four important values:

1. People and communication are more important than strict processes and tools.
2. A working product is more important than extensive documentation.
3. Collaboration with customers is more important than negotiating contracts.
4. Being able to respond to change is more important than strictly following a plan.

These values encourage teams to focus on communication, collaboration, and delivering useful products.


### Scrum vs Kanban

Scrum and Kanban are two common Agile frameworks.
Scrum works in fixed time periods called **sprints**, usually lasting about 2–4 weeks. At the start of each sprint, the team plans what tasks they will complete. Scrum also has specific roles such as Scrum Master and Product Owner.

Kanban is more flexible. Instead of working in sprints, tasks move continuously through different stages such as **To Do**, **In Progress**, and **Done**. Team members can pick up new tasks as soon as they finish their current ones.

In simple terms, **Scrum focuses on structured cycles**, while **Kanban focuses on continuous workflow and flexibility**.


### Why Focus Bear Uses Kanban

Focus Bear likely prefers Kanban because it allows work to be updated and prioritized quickly. For example, when improving a website, tasks such as fixing bugs, updating content, or adding small features can appear at any time.
Using Kanban makes it easier to manage these tasks because work can be added and completed continuously without waiting for a new sprint to start.

## Reflection
### Benefits and Challenges of Agile

One major benefit of Agile is flexibility. Teams can quickly adapt if requirements change. Agile also encourages regular communication, which helps team members stay aligned.
Another benefit is that improvements can be delivered continuously instead of waiting until the end of a long project.

However, Agile can also have challenges. It requires good communication and coordination between team members. Without clear organization, tasks can become confusing or priorities may change too often.


### How Agile improve my role and responsibilities

As a Website Developer Intern, Agile helps me stay organized and understand what tasks need to be done. Using a Kanban board makes it easy to see which tasks are waiting to be done, which ones are in progress, and which ones are completed.
This helps me manage my work more effectively and allows the team to track progress clearly.
