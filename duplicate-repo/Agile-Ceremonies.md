# Agile Ceremonies & Team Collaboration

## Research & Learn

### Agile ceremonies
#### Daily Stand-ups
Daily stand-ups are short meetings where team members briefly share updates about their work. Each person usually answers three questions: what they worked on yesterday, what they plan to work on today, and if there are any blockers. The purpose is to keep everyone informed and aligned on progress.

#### Sprint Planning (Scrum) vs Continuous Prioritization (Kanban)

**Sprint Planning (Scrum)**  
In Scrum teams, sprint planning is used to decide which tasks will be completed during the upcoming sprint. The team reviews the backlog and selects tasks that can be completed within the sprint timeframe.

**Continuous Prioritization (Kanban)**  
In Kanban, work is not organized into sprints. Instead, tasks are continuously prioritized and added to the workflow as needed. Team members pick tasks from the board when they have capacity.

#### Retrospectives
A retrospective is a meeting where the team reflects on how the previous sprint or work period went. The team discusses what worked well, what did not work well, and what improvements can be made for the next cycle.

#### Backlog Refinement
Backlog refinement is the process of reviewing and updating tasks in the backlog. This includes clarifying requirements, estimating effort, and ensuring tasks are ready to be worked on.


### Collaboration across time zones

Agile teams often collaborate asynchronously, especially when working across different time zones. Communication tools such as Slack, GitHub, and project boards allow team members to share updates without needing to be online at the same time. Clear documentation and regular updates help ensure that everyone stays informed.

## Reflection

### How Agile ceremonies help communication
Agile ceremonies help improve communication within the team by providing regular opportunities to share updates and discuss progress. They ensure that everyone understands the current priorities and any challenges the team is facing. This helps keep the team aligned and reduces misunderstandings.

### Most important ceremony for my role
For my role as a Website Developer Intern, daily stand-ups are particularly important. They allow me to share progress on my tasks and quickly mention any blockers I encounter. Stand-ups also help me understand what other team members are working on and how my tasks fit into the larger project.
