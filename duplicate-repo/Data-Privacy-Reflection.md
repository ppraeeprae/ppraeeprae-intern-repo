# Data Privacy & Confidentiality Reflection

## Research & Learn
### Key takeaways from Focus Bear’s Privacy Policy

After reviewing Focus Bear’s privacy policy, I learned that the company collects certain types of user data in order to provide and improve its services. This may include information related to user profiles, routines, and usage of the application.

The policy emphasizes that personal data must be protected from unauthorized access and misuse. Focus Bear also limits how user data is shared and only uses necessary information for specific purposes.

Another important point is that users have rights over their personal data, such as requesting access to their information, correcting it, or requesting its deletion.

### Types of confidential data at Focus Bear

Examples of confidential data include:

- Personal user information
- Account details
- Habit and routine data
- Internal company documents
- System credentials such as passwords or API keys

This information must be handled carefully and only accessed by authorized team members.

### Best practices for handling confidential data

To protect confidential information, several best practices should be followed:

- Use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication
- Avoid sharing sensitive information in public platforms
- Only access data necessary for the task
- Store information in secure systems
- Lock devices when not in use

Following these practices helps reduce the risk of data leaks and protects user privacy.

### Responding to a data breach

If a data breach or accidental disclosure occurs, it should be reported immediately to the appropriate team or supervisor. Quick reporting allows the company to investigate the issue and take action to protect affected data.

Changing passwords and securing affected systems may also be necessary to prevent further damage.

## Reflection

### Handling data securely in my daily tasks

In my role as a Website Developer Intern, I will make sure that I only access the information required for my work. I will avoid storing sensitive information on personal devices and ensure that my devices are locked when I step away.

### Storing, Sharing, and Disposing of sensitive information

Sensitive information should be stored in secure systems and shared only with authorized team members. When information is no longer needed, it should be safely deleted according to company policies.

### Common mistakes that lead to privacy issues

Common mistakes include using weak passwords, sharing confidential information in public repositories, or accidentally exposing sensitive data online.

These mistakes can be avoided by carefully reviewing information before sharing it and following company security guidelines.
