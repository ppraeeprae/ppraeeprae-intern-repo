# Occupational Health & Safety (OHS) for Desk-Based Work

## Research & Learn

### Risks of using a laptop without external equipment

Working directly on a laptop for long periods can create several ergonomic risks. Because the keyboard and screen are attached, it is difficult to position both at the correct height at the same time. This often causes people to bend their neck downward or hunch their shoulders.
Some common risks include:
- Neck and shoulder strain from looking down at the screen
- Wrist discomfort due to poor keyboard positioning
- Lower back pain caused by poor sitting posture
- Eye strain from focusing on a screen for long periods without breaks
These issues can lead to repetitive strain injuries and reduced productivity.


### Ergonomic equipment for better posture

- **Laptop stand** – raises the screen to eye level to reduce neck strain
- **External keyboard and mouse** – allows hands and wrists to stay in a more natural position
- **Ergonomic chair** – provides proper back support
- **External monitor** – makes it easier to maintain correct screen height and distance
Using even one or two of these tools can significantly improve comfort during long work sessions.


### Recommended workspace setup

A healthy desk setup should follow a few ergonomic guidelines:
- The top of the screen should be roughly at eye level
- The screen should be about an arm’s length away
- The chair should support the lower back
- Feet should rest flat on the floor
- Elbows should stay at about a 90-degree angle when typing


### Habits that reduce strain

Good habits are just as important as good equipment. Some useful daily practices include:
- Taking short movement breaks every 30–60 minutes
- Doing quick posture checks throughout the day
- Following the 20-20-20 rule to reduce eye strain  
  (every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds)
- Stretching the neck, shoulders, and wrists during breaks

### Workspace equipment changes

To improve my workspace setup, I can use a laptop stand to raise my screen closer to eye level. This would help reduce the tendency to bend my neck while working.
Using an external keyboard and mouse would also allow my hands and wrists to stay in a more comfortable typing position.


### Behavioural changes

To reduce strain and improve posture, I plan to:
- Sit upright with my back supported by the chair
- Keep my screen closer to eye level
- Take short breaks to stretch and move around
- Avoid working in the same position for long periods
These small changes can help reduce discomfort during long study or work sessions.


### Reminders and movement breaks

To maintain good posture and take regular breaks, I can use Focus Bear to schedule movement reminders throughout the day.
For example, I can set reminders every 30–45 minutes to:
- Stand up
- Stretch my shoulders and neck
- Look away from the screen briefly
Using these reminders helps build healthier work habits and prevents long periods of sitting.
