# Roles in an Agile Team

## Research & Learn

### Agile team
#### Developers
Developers are responsible for building and maintaining the software product. They write code, implement features, fix bugs, and ensure the application works correctly. Developers collaborate with designers, QA, and product managers to deliver working software.

#### QA (Quality Assurance)
QA ensures that the product works as expected. They test new features, identify bugs, and verify that the system meets quality standards before release.

#### Product Manager (PM)
The Product Manager defines the product vision and priorities. They manage the product backlog, decide which features should be developed, and ensure the product delivers value to users.

#### Agile Project Manager (Agile PM)
The Agile Project Manager helps coordinate the development process. They facilitate team communication, remove blockers, and ensure the team follows Agile practices such as stand-ups and sprint planning.

#### Other Supporting Roles

**Stakeholders**  
Stakeholders provide input on product goals and business needs.

**UX Designers**  
UX designers focus on improving user experience and ensuring the interface is easy to use.

**Customer Support**  
Customer support collects feedback from users and reports issues to the development team.


### Kanban vs Scrum Collaboration
#### Scrum
In Scrum, work is organized into fixed time periods called sprints. The team has structured meetings such as sprint planning, daily stand-ups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives. Each role works together to deliver a set of planned tasks during the sprint.

#### Kanban
Kanban focuses on continuous delivery. Work items move across a visual board (e.g., To Do, In Progress, Done). There are fewer strict roles, and team members collaborate flexibly to complete tasks.

My role is a Website Developer Intern. I contribute by developing and improving website features, fixing issues, and helping maintain the performance and usability of the website. My work supports the team by implementing tasks from the backlog and ensuring that features are delivered efficiently.
